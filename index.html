Where the 5% Active Rate
Is Being Lost
One stage dominates. This is a first transaction problem.
Start → KYC complete 40% Intentional quality filter. Not a problem to fix.
KYC complete → Card requested 60% Reasonable 2 months post-migration.
Card issued → First transaction 20% Primary lever. This IS the active rate.
First transaction → Repeat same month 40% Fixable once first transaction is solved.
HIGH IMPACT · H1
Card issued, wallet empty
66% of card failures are insufficient balance. Users tap with no money in the wallet. Each failed auth has a unit cost.

Fix: auto-freeze after 3 consecutive declines. Strategic fix: credit product removes pre-funding entirely.
HIGH IMPACT · H2
Issuance moment wastes peak intent
User just completed KYC — peak intent. Card is issued immediately with €0 wallet. Appears active. Declines on every tap.

Fix: gate the card. Fund first, card reveal is the reward. No milestone moment is wasted.
H3 · Medium
No clear first use case. Transport is the natural anchor — habitual, low value, no partnership needed.
H4 · Medium
Virtual card UX unfamiliar. CVC2 failures (202), online PIN exceeded (121). Fix at point of failure.
H5 · Medium
Apple Pay errors (339). Likely manual card entry, not native provisioning. Native flow removes this category.

Test the Gate.
Measure What Predicts the Number.
One focused A/B test. Leading indicators that move before the monthly report does.
Hypothesis: If we require wallet funding before card issuance, 7-day first-transaction rate will increase from 20% to ≥28% — because users arrive at card issuance with money already in the wallet.

Current flow
Card issued with €0 wallet. Declines on every transaction attempt. No prompt, no urgency.
Treatment
Fund First — Card as Reward
Top-up prompt post-KYC. Linked e-wallet or bank transfer. Card only issued after wallet is funded. Consistent with full KYC quality-first philosophy.
