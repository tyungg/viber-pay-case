01 / 04 — Diagnosis
Where the 5% Active Rate Is Being Lost
Four compounding drop-offs — but one stage dominates. The active rate is a first transaction problem.
The Funnel
StageRateRead
Start → KYC complete 40% Intentional quality filter. Deliberate move to full KYC. Not a problem to fix.
KYC complete → Card requested 60% Reasonable for a product 2 months post-migration.
Card issued → First transaction 20% This IS the active rate. Primary lever.
First transaction → Repeat same month 40% Fixable once first transaction is solved.
H1 — Card issued, wallet empty
HIGH · Low effort
STAGE: Card issued → First transaction
Transaction data: 66% of all card failures are insufficient balance. Intentional low-balance behaviour — users tap hopefully without funding first. Each failed auth has a unit cost.
  • Auto-freeze after 3 consecutive declines — user must actively unfreeze. Reduces auth cost, signals the problem back to the user, and prompts top-up at the unfreeze step.
  • Strategic fix: A credit product removes pre-funding entirely. User spends against a credit line, repays later. Eliminates root cause. Roadmap item.
H2 — Issuance moment wastes peak intent
HIGH · Low effort
STAGE: Card issued → First transaction
User just completed KYC — most effortful step in the funnel. Peak intent. Current flow issues the card immediately with no funding gate — card appears active but declines on every transaction.
  • Gate the card: Virtual card not issued until wallet has a balance. Top-up first, card reveal is the reward. No milestone moment is wasted.
  • Validate: Time between KYC completion and first top-up attempt. Card issuance completion rate delta.
Further signals — transaction data · prioritised Impact × Effort
H3 — No first use case [MED · First transaction]: Transport is the natural anchor — habitual, low value, contactless accepted on EU transit already. No partnership needed.
H4 — Virtual card UX unfamiliar [MED · First transaction]: CVC2 failures (202), online PIN attempts (121). Each error needs a 1-tap in-context fix at point of failure.
H5 — Apple Pay errors (339) [MED · First transaction]: Likely manual card entry into Apple Wallet rather than native provisioning. Native provisioning flow removes this failure category.
Sequencing: H3–5 are Medium Impact, Medium Effort. Address after H1 and H2 are live and measured.

Screen 1 · Top-Up First
  • "Add money to get your Viber Pay card" — card is the reward for funding
  • Bottom sheet — minimal taps
  • Linked e-wallet — primary CTA (instant)
  • Bank transfer — secondary option
  • €10 pre-selected, editable
Screen 2 · Card Reveal
  • Full colour Viber Pay card revealed — user's name, full Viber purple
  • "Your Viber Pay card is ready"
  • Card is the reward for topping up — not a gate to get past
  • Trust line: "Regulated in the EU. Funds protected."
Screen 3 · Reward + First Use
  • Reward unlock: Viber Pay profile badge + exclusive sticker pack
  • First transaction prompt: Buy a sticker pack for €1.99 with your new Viber Pay card — card-exclusive offer
  • Retention hook: 50% off next sticker pack after purchase — creates a reason to return and spend again
  • Single CTA: "Buy stickers · €1.99 →"
Trust in a messaging app: Users sharing financial data inside a messaging app need explicit reassurance. Three design principles: (1) Viber Pay screens use a distinct visual treatment — clearly separated from chat UI. (2) Every financial screen shows the regulatory line ("Regulated in the EU · Your funds are protected by a licensed EU institution"). (3) Biometric authentication (Face ID / fingerprint) is required before any card details are shown — same as a banking app, not a messaging app.
P2P activation is a parallel track — designed and measured independently from card activation.

03 / 04 — Engagement
Day 0–7: From Card Issued
to Habit Formed
5 touchpoints, 3 channels, intentional rest days. Assumes current flow — card already issued, wallet may or may not be funded. Every notification deep-links to exactly the right screen.
DayChannelMessageRationale
Day 0 In-flow Card issued — prompt to add money and use your card. Top-up sheet surfaced immediately post-issuance. No notification needed if user completes inline. Peak intent at issuance — capture it in the moment before it dissipates.
Day 2, 4, 6—Rest days — avoid opt-out fatigue—
Day 1 Viber msg "💳 Your Viber Pay card is ready — add money and make your first purchase today." From Viber Pay system contact. Quick-reply: "Add money now" → top-up sheet · "How it works" → inline guide. Arrives in existing chat list, not as a push. Higher open rate. User already in Viber context.
Day 3 In-chat "Send money to [Name] right here →" Banner in chat with a Viber Pay contact. Only shown if contact has Viber Pay. Real social moment — user has a concrete reason to top up and use the card right now.
Day 5 Push "[X] of your Viber contacts are already using Viber Pay — don't miss out." Personalised contact count pulled from user's Viber graph. Social proof over loss aversion. Personalised count feels relevant, not generic. Push reaches users not actively in Viber.
Day 7 Push + in-app "🚇 Use your Viber Pay card on public transport today — we'll cover your first ride." Transit-only credit (MCC 4111) loaded to card. Non-activated users only. Specific use case over generic cashback. Ties directly to the transit first-use framing. Day 7 timing avoids incentivising users who would have activated anyway.
Channel logic
  • Viber messages (Day 1, 3) — arrive in existing chat list, not as a push notification. Higher open rate. Native deep-linking to Pay tab. Best for users actively using Viber.
  • Push notifications (Day 5, 7) — reach users not actively opening Viber. Broader reach, lower conversion. Used for re-engagement only.
  • In-chat contextual (Day 3) — highest intent, lowest reach. Only surfaces when a real social trigger exists (Viber Pay contact in active chat).

04 / 04 — Measure & Test
Test the Funding Gate.
Measure What Predicts the Monthly Number.
One focused A/B test on the highest-leverage intervention. Leading indicators that move weeks before the monthly report.
A/B Test — Fund First vs Current Flow
Hypothesis: If we require users to fund their wallet before the virtual card is issued, first card transaction rate within 7 days will increase from 20% to ≥28% — because users arrive at card issuance with money already in the wallet, removing the primary reason for first-transaction failure.
Control
Current Experience
KYC complete → virtual card issued immediately → user lands on €0 wallet. Card appears active but declines on every transaction attempt. No prompt, no urgency, no forward action.
Treatment
Fund First — Card as Reward
KYC complete → top-up prompt (linked e-wallet or bank transfer, €10 pre-selected) → wallet funded → virtual card issued and revealed as a milestone reward. Card is only issued after the wallet has a balance. Consistent with full KYC philosophy — optimising for quality over volume.
